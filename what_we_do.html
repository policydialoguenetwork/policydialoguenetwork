What we do

We at PDN aim to enrich the dialogue on policymaking and relevant domains by initiating in-depth participatory research through networking, collaborations and citizen participation. We strive to ensure that policymaking is strengthened and complemented by extensive discussions, debates and analytical research.

The NETWORKING endeavour at PDN works towards providing a platform for policy enthusiasts and practitioners, thus creating a culture of policy discussion among researchers. By bringing together these young research aspirants and scholars from across the country, the attempt is to create an extensive and diverse network.

Engaging with researchers who are well versed with the current trends and debates in policymaking, PDN brings in a fresh perspective. The experience and expertise of various actors in the network promise to make space for dynamic discussions on policymaking. As experts and specialists in their domains, they enrich the network diversity. Through providing a platform for dialogue, PDN enhances communication among researchers enabling collaborations, thereby fostering better and more effective research practices and outputs.

The research and dialogue that PDN initiates through the network get complemented by the COLLABORATIONS we engage in. The direct collaborations with the network of researchers and partnerships with organizations, research institutes and governments are the two aspects of PDN's collaboration initiative. The in-house team engages with these actors at the two ends occupying the centre stage, producing research articles, policy reports and recommendations.

As collaborators, the in-house team and the network work on an array of projects envisioned by either of the partakers, creating a space for nurturing innovative and novel research. Also, it augments the quality of policy research through promoting interdisciplinary teams of domain specialists. At the other end, the team works out partnerships to cater to outsourced work by the government and engage as ground-level research partners to other organizations and research institutes.

PDN acknowledges citizen experience as relevant to public policy and therefore values CITIZEN PARTICIPATION in policymaking. Deriving from this idea, our work involves channelling citizen opinion in drafting policy recommendations. In an attempt to understand the implications of policy decisions for the citizens, we aspire to gather their feedback on policy decisions through a transparent polling system made possible with the aid of technology.

In our efforts at networking, collaboration and enhancing citizen participation, we attempt to bridge the gap between policy and research. We aim to achieve the larger goal of democratizing the policy space through diversifying and increasing the number of actors.
